# Kyru Multi-Location Management
## Quick Start Guide

**Get started with Multi-Location Dashboard, Recipe Comparison, and Vendor Pricing**

---

## What is Kyru Multi-Location Management?

Kyru Multi-Location Management is a set of tools designed to help restaurant owners and managers with 2-3 locations see how each location is performing, compare costs between locations, and identify opportunities to save money.

Instead of managing each location separately, you now have one centralized dashboard where you can see which location is most efficient, why it's more efficient, and how to replicate that success across all your locations.

---

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Log Into Your Account
1. Go to https://aapp-final-1.vercel.app
2. Sign in with your email and password
3. You should see your restaurant name at the top

### Step 2: Navigate to Multi-Location Dashboard
1. On the left sidebar, click **"Multi-Location"**
2. You'll see cards for each of your locations displayed side-by-side
3. Each card shows key performance metrics

### Step 3: Understand the Color Coding
- **Green** = Great performance (keep doing what you're doing)
- **Yellow** = Watch this metric (could improve)
- **Red** = Action needed (this needs attention)

### Step 4: Explore the Tabs
You'll see three tabs at the top:
- **Overview** (current page) - See all metrics for all locations
- **Recipe Comparison** - Compare how much recipes cost at each location
- **Vendor Pricing** - See which location negotiated the best price with vendors

## Multi-Location Dashboard: What You're Seeing

### The Location Cards
Each location shows 5 key metrics:

**Food Cost %** — What percentage of your revenue goes to food
- Green: Less than 30%
- Yellow: 30-34%
- Red: More than 34%

**Labor Cost %** — What percentage of your revenue goes to labor
- Green: Less than 25%
- Yellow: 25-28%
- Red: More than 28%

**Prime Cost %** — Food + Labor combined (the two biggest costs)
- Green: Less than 55%
- Yellow: 55-60%
- Red: More than 60%

**Inventory Accuracy %** — How accurately your inventory counts match what the system shows
- Green: 95%+ (excellent)
- Yellow: 80-95% (good, but room to improve)
- Red: Below 80% (needs attention)

**Revenue (Last 30 Days)** — Total sales in the last month

### Best Performers Section
At the bottom, you'll see which location is doing best for each metric:
- **Lowest Food Cost** — Which location has the best food cost percentage
- **Lowest Labor Cost** — Which location has the best labor percentage
- **Best Prime Cost** — Which location is most efficient overall

The system also shows you the dollar gap: "Location A is $0.35 cheaper per unit than Location B"

### Clicking on a Location
Click any location card to see that location's individual dashboard with more detailed information.

## Recipe Comparison: Finding Cost Differences

### What It Does
Let's say you make a Margarita at all three locations. But what if it costs $2.10 to make at Location A, but $2.45 at Location B? Recipe Comparison shows you why.

### How to Use It

**Step 1:** Click the **"Recipe Comparison"** tab

**Step 2:** Click the dropdown that says "SELECT RECIPE"
- You'll see a list of recipes available at multiple locations
- Pick one (for example, "Margarita")

**Step 3:** You'll see three cards side-by-side, one for each location

**Step 4:** Look at the breakdown
- **Recipe Cost:** How much it costs to make
- **Cost %:** What percentage of the $20 selling price
- **Ingredients section:** Shows each ingredient and its cost

### Real Example
**Margarita at three locations, selling price $20:**

Location A: $2.10 cost (10.5%)
- Club Soda: 3 oz @ $0.21/oz = $0.63
- Lime: 0.5 each @ $0.30 = $0.15
- Tequila: 1.5 oz @ $0.85/oz = $1.28

Location B: $2.45 cost (12.3%)
- Club Soda: 3 oz @ $0.25/oz = $0.75 ← More expensive!
- Lime: 0.5 each @ $0.35 = $0.18
- Tequila: 1.5 oz @ $0.95/oz = $1.43

Location C: $2.30 cost (11.5%)
- Club Soda: 3 oz @ $0.22/oz = $0.66
- Lime: 0.5 each @ $0.32 = $0.16
- Tequila: 1.5 oz @ $0.90/oz = $1.35

**What to do:** Location B is paying more for Club Soda ($0.25/oz vs $0.21/oz). Ask Location A's manager: "Who are you buying Club Soda from?" Then switch Location B's vendor.

## Vendor Pricing: Finding the Best Deals

### What It Does
You buy chicken from multiple vendors across your locations. Maybe Location A pays $4.20/lb, but Location B negotiated $3.85/lb with a different vendor. Vendor Pricing shows you these differences and calculates how much you'd save if you consolidated.

### How to Use It

**Step 1:** Click the **"Vendor Pricing"** tab

**Step 2:** Click the dropdown that says "SELECT ITEM"
- Items are listed by how much you spend on them (highest first)
- Pick one (for example, "Chicken")

**Step 3:** You'll see vendor information for each location
- Vendor name
- Price per unit
- Date of last purchase

**Step 4:** Look for savings opportunities
- The app shows you the cheapest price across locations
- It calculates: "If all locations bought from the cheapest vendor, you'd save $X/month ($Y/year)"

### Real Example
**Chicken across three locations:**

Location A: $4.20/lb from Supplier X
- 200 lbs/month
- $840/month

Location B: $3.85/lb from Supplier Y ← Best price!
- 200 lbs/month
- $770/month

Location C: $4.10/lb from Supplier X
- 200 lbs/month
- $820/month

**Total across all locations:** 600 lbs/month, $2,430/month spend

**If all locations bought from Supplier Y at $3.85/lb:** 600 × $3.85 = $2,310/month

**Savings:** $2,430 - $2,310 = **$120/month = $1,440/year** (just on chicken!)

**What to do:** 
1. Call Supplier Y and ask if they can supply all three locations
2. Negotiate with Supplier X using Supplier Y's price as leverage
3. The best outcome: all three locations get the $3.85 rate

## Quick Tips & Best Practices

### 1. Check Your Dashboard Weekly
Every Monday morning, spend 5 minutes checking which location has red flags. A red metric means something changed — investigate it.

### 2. Use Recipe Comparison Before Changing Recipes
Don't change a recipe because it seems expensive. Use Recipe Comparison to see if another location makes it cheaper. If so, ask them how.

### 3. Vendor Pricing is a Negotiation Tool
When you see a competitor location negotiated a better price, use that data in your next vendor call. "Location B is getting chicken for $3.85/lb. Can you match that?"

### 4. Share Best Practices
If Location A has the best food cost, have their manager talk to the other locations. What are they doing differently?

### 5. Track Changes Over Time
Don't expect changes overnight. If you switch vendors or adjust recipes, give it 30 days, then check the metrics again.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why does my food cost % seem high?**
A: Compare it to your other locations using the Multi-Location Dashboard. If Location A's food cost is 28% and Location B's is 32%, ask Location A what they're doing differently. Maybe it's vendor pricing, maybe it's portion control, maybe it's waste management.

**Q: Can I change recipes mid-month?**
A: Yes. As soon as you enter new invoices with different ingredients, the Recipe Comparison tool will update automatically. It uses the most recent pricing data.

**Q: How far back does the data go?**
A: The Multi-Location Dashboard shows the last 30 days. Recipe Comparison and Vendor Pricing use the most recent invoices from the last 30 days to calculate costs.

**Q: What if one location doesn't have a recipe the others have?**
A: That recipe won't show up in Recipe Comparison yet. Add the recipe to that location first, then it will appear in the comparison.

**Q: Can I use this data to negotiate with vendors?**
A: Absolutely! That's the whole point. If you see another location negotiated a better price, use that in your conversation with the vendor.

**Q: What should I do if my prime cost is above 60%?**
A: Prime Cost is food + labor combined. If you're above 60%, you need to improve either food cost or labor cost (or both). Use Recipe Comparison to find cheaper recipes, and use Vendor Pricing to negotiate better prices.

**Q: How often should I review these metrics?**
A: Weekly is ideal. Check every Monday morning. Monthly deep-dives are good too — sit down with your location managers and discuss what changed and why.
